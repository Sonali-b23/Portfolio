DESIGN AND ANALYSIS OF ANFIS-CONTROLLED MPPT FOR SOLAR PV SYSTEMS

Problem

Solar PV systems often fail to maximize energy extraction due to changing environmental conditions, leading to inefficiencies.

Approach

  • System Design: Developed an ANFIS-based controller for optimal operating point adjustment.
  • Simulation: Modeled the system in MATLAB/Simulink to evaluate performance.
  • Performance Evaluation: Compared ANFIS-controlled MPPT with traditional methods.

Results

  • Enhanced Efficiency: Achieved a 20% improvement in energy extraction.
  • Quick Adaptation: Faster response to changing irradiance conditions.
  • Robust Performance: Stable operation across various scenarios.

This project highlights the advantages of using ANFIS for improving solar energy capture in PV systems.

DESIGNING AN ECO-FRIENDLY HYBRID VEHICLE PROTOTYPE

Problem

The growing need for sustainable transportation demands hybrid vehicles that reduce environmental impact without sacrificing performance, as current designs often lead to higher emissions and resource use.

Approach

  • Research & Analysis: Review existing hybrid technologies and eco-friendly materials.
  • Design Phase: Use CAD software to create a prototype with lightweight materials and an efficient powertrain.
  • Simulation: Model vehicle performance to enhance fuel efficiency and lower emissions.
  • Sustainability Assessment: Conduct a lifecycle assessment of materials and manufacturing processes.

Results

  • Enhanced Efficiency: Improved fuel efficiency over traditional hybrids.
  • Reduced Emissions: 30% decrease in greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Positive Feedback: High satisfaction in initial user testing regarding performance and comfort.

This project underscores the viability of eco-friendly designs in advancing sustainable transportation.
